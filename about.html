My Journey

I've always gravitated toward technology; as a child I spent countless hours dialing into the internet, wondering how it all worked. I first discovered web development early in high school, following Dreamweaver tutorials from outdated textbooks on iMacs that had seen better years. It was there I began to see the potential for what could be created on the web, untethered from a WYSIWYG application. I began learning the beginnings of writing real html and css and realized that a career doing this could be for me.

In college I started out as a computer science major and quickly learned that it wasn't for me. I respected the theory, understood why learning it was necessary, but never enjoyed it. It seemed like nothing ever actually got made - the professor would give a lecture and you would write a small program outputting to the terminal. I then discovered the Interactive Multimedia program at my college and immediately jumped ship. In the IMM program I learned theory, yes, but I also had the satisfaction of seeing immediate results. I thrived on creating things. I learned front-end web dev in-depth, along with other skills like digital storytelling, technical writing, and game design.

My education allowed me the luxury of learning dozens of relevant and interesting technologies and provided a strong base for further self-learning, which I continue to take advantage of through courses on sites like Udemy and LinkedIn Learning.
